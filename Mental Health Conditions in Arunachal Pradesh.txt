<h1>Mental Health in Arunachal Pradesh</h1>

<p>
<h3>1. Psychological Disorders in Arunachal Pradesh and Comorbidities</h3>
In Arunachal Pradesh, prevalent psychological disorders include depression, schizophrenia, and bipolar disorder, often coexisting with depression, anxiety, and substance <br> abuse disorders:
<br>
- Depression: Prevalent due to social isolation and economic challenges, often comorbid with anxiety and substance abuse.
<br>
- Anxiety: Widespread among the youth and elderly, frequently occurring with depression.
<br>
- Substance Abuse: High rates of alcohol and drug use, leading to significant mental health issues.
</p>

<p>
<h3>2. Causes of Rising Mental Health Issues</h3>
Factors contributing to mental health issues in Arunachal Pradesh include:
<br>
- Cultural Stress: Changes in traditional lifestyles and cultural disintegration.
<br>
- Economic Hardship: Poverty and lack of employment opportunities.
<br>
- Substance Abuse: Significant problem leading to increased mental health issues.
<br>
- Geographic Isolation: Difficult terrain and poor connectivity affecting access to healthcare.
</p>
</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<p>
<h3>3. Mental Health Care Facilities and Resources in Arunachal Pradesh</h3>
- Hospitals and Clinics: Tomo Riba Institute of Health and Medical Sciences, various district hospitals, and primary health centers.
<br>
- Rehabilitation Centers: State-run and private rehab centers for substance abuse.
<br>
- NGOs: Active NGOs like the Arunachal Pali Vidyapith Society providing counseling and support.
<br>
- Helplines: Mental health helplines and crisis intervention services available.
</p>
</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<p>
<h3>4. Government Initiatives for Preventive Awareness</h3>
- Central Initiatives: Implementation of NMHP, Ayushman Bharat scheme, and Manodarpan initiative.
<br>
- State Initiatives: Mental health awareness campaigns, community outreach programs, and training for healthcare providers.
<br>
- Educational Initiatives: Mental health education integrated into school curriculums, workshops for educators and students.
</p>

<p>
<h3>5. Comparative Analysis with Delhi</h3>
- Prevalence: Arunachal Pradesh faces higher challenges due to geographic isolation and cultural stress compared to Delhi.
<br>
- Facilities: Delhi has more comprehensive and accessible mental health services.
<br>
- Government Support: Both regions receive central government support, but Delhi benefits from more robust local programs.
<br>
While Delhi has a higher prevalence of lifestyle-related disorders, Arunachal Pradesh contends with issues like lack of access to care and mental health awareness.
